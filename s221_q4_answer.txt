Section 2.2.1 Question 4 – Convergence process (6 neurons, 2 hidden layers)

Compared to the 16-neuron case:

Loss vs iteration:
  The loss still decreases and converges, but the final loss value may be higher (the model
  with 6 neurons has less capacity). The convergence curve may be less smooth, with small
  fluctuations, because fewer neurons means each one carries more gradient signal and the
  loss landscape is less well-conditioned.

Gradient norm vs iteration:
  Similar overall shape to the 16-neuron case, but may not reach as small a final gradient
  norm, since the reduced capacity means the model cannot fully minimize the loss.

Animation:
  The decision boundary animation shows simpler, coarser boundaries compared to 16 neurons.
  The boundaries are piecewise linear but with fewer pieces (fewer neurons = fewer linear
  segments per boundary). The final boundary still separates the classes reasonably well but
  is less tightly fitted to the spiral structure.

Explanation:
  With only 6 neurons per hidden layer, the network has limited expressive power. It can
  represent fewer piecewise-linear regions in the input space, so the decision boundary is
  cruder. The model may underfit slightly compared to the 16-neuron version. This illustrates
  the trade-off between network width and model expressiveness.
