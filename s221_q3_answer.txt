Section 2.2.1 Question 3 – Convergence process (16 neurons, 2 hidden layers)

Loss vs iteration:
  The loss decreases steadily from its initial value and converges to near zero, following a
  roughly exponential decay curve. There are no sharp oscillations, indicating that eta=0.1
  is a stable learning rate for this problem size.

Gradient norm vs iteration:
  The gradient norm is large initially (the network is far from the optimum), decreases
  monotonically, and approaches zero as training converges. The shape mirrors the loss curve.

Animation:
  The decision boundary animation shows that early on (first few hundred iterations) the
  boundaries are near-linear and roughly symmetric, reflecting random initial weights.
  As training progresses, the boundaries bend and wrap around the spiral arms, becoming
  more curved and precise. By the end of training, the boundaries tightly follow the three
  spirals, separating them cleanly.

This illustrates how gradient descent progressively refines the network's representation:
early iterations learn coarse structure, later iterations fine-tune boundary placement.
